# Whisper Beneath the Lighthouse — Expanded Story Outline

## Purpose

This document defines an expanded version of the existing **Whisper Beneath the Lighthouse** test story for Narrium.

The expansion should make the story substantially longer, deepen the characters, add more investigation and dialogue, and create more visible consequences for player decisions. It must preserve the current narrative core and end in approximately the same four world states so that the planned continuation routes remain valid.

All story content, scene names, narration, dialogue, choices, conditions, hints, resource names, and variable-facing text in the future expanded Narrium export should be written in English.

## Current baseline

The existing version already provides a strong test of Narrium's branching logic, but it is narratively compressed. Most routes move from the first discovery to the finale in roughly six to eight played scenes, and many scenes contain only four to six dialogue pages.

The expanded version should retain:

- Lio, Mira, Father Cael, Eryn Voss, and Noa;
- the current resources and character attributes unless balancing requires small adjustments;
- the central truth that the lighthouse does not simply repel the fog;
- the four existing endings;
- compatibility with all four continuation blueprints;
- the current dark-fantasy, maritime, painterly tone of Veyr.

## Narrative goals

The expanded story should:

1. Establish daily life in Veyr before the central mystery accelerates.
2. Give Lio stronger personal reasons to investigate.
3. Make Mira's search for her brother emotionally central.
4. Show Father Cael's guilt through actions rather than exposition alone.
5. Present Voss as a morally compromised protector rather than a simple villain.
6. Make Noa mysterious without fully explaining her origin.
7. Separate the investigation into historical, human, and supernatural evidence.
8. Let earlier choices visibly affect the final night.
9. Preserve the four ending states required by the sequel routes.

## Target size

Recommended production target:

- approximately 26 scenes in total;
- approximately 150–190 dialogue pages;
- approximately 13–17 scenes in an average playthrough;
- four endings;
- several route-specific scenes before the Lighthouse Heart;
- approximately 60–90 minutes for one playthrough;
- approximately 3–4 hours to see most significant variants.

The story should not add a fifth ending. The current four endings already cover the essential thematic outcomes:

- truth;
- order;
- escape;
- sacrifice and transformation.

# ACT I — The Port and the First Secret

The first act should establish Veyr as a living city, show Lio's place in it, and gradually turn unease into suspicion.

## S01 — The Archive After Midnight

Keep the current opening scene, but expand it.

### New material

- Lio speaks with an elderly archivist named Iven before Iven leaves for the night.
- Iven mentions missing registers from the Lower Quay.
- Lio hears footsteps on an upper floor even though the building is locked.
- Alongside the untitled ledger, Lio finds a torn transport manifest.
- The lighthouse subtly reacts when Lio touches the document.
- The ledger still contains the central sentence:

> The lighthouse does not drive the fog away. The lighthouse feeds it.

### Existing final choice structure

- Hide the ledger beneath the coat.
- Leave it and pretend nothing happened.
- Copy only the final page.

These choices should continue to affect Truth, Calm, Voss's suspicion, Lio's insight, and Lio's guilt.

## S02 — The Walk Through the Harbor — New Scene

Lio returns home through the harbor at night.

### Purpose

- Show Veyr before the investigation begins.
- Introduce the behavior of the fog at street level.
- Create the first direct interaction with the city guard.
- Suggest that the document carried by Lio affects the fog.

### Events

- A guard patrol stops workers and checks travel papers.
- A lantern goes dark when Lio approaches.
- The fog gathers near the ledger or copied page.
- Lio notices a covered cart leaving the Lower Quay.

### Choices

1. Submit to inspection and rely on official credentials.
2. Avoid the patrol through the warehouses.
3. Pay a ferryman to cross a canal unnoticed.

### Possible effects

- Reputation changes.
- Supplies change.
- Voss suspicion changes.
- Lio courage changes.
- A hidden flag may record whether the patrol saw Lio carrying documents.

## S03 — Dream of the Sunken Bell — New Scene

A short surreal dream sequence.

### Events

- Lio stands on the seabed beneath a suspended version of Veyr.
- A bell rings without moving.
- A child's voice says:

> Do not wake the light. It is dreaming through you.

- A pale beam searches the drowned streets.

### Function

- Foreshadow Noa.
- Foreshadow the underwater vision.
- Increase Insight or reduce Calm depending on the response.
- Create conditional dialogue when Lio later meets Noa.

## S04 — The Market in the Fog

Keep the current route-selection scene, but expand the city activity.

### New material

- A family argues with the guard about a missing fisherman.
- Merchants close stalls despite the early hour.
- Mira gives Lio a coded warning before addressing him openly.
- Father Cael performs a salt ritual, but one protected doorway immediately becomes wet again.
- Lio notices that the guard collects possessions belonging to missing people instead of searching for them.

### Final choices

- Follow Mira into a side street.
- Approach Father Cael.
- Stay and observe the guard.

These remain the main entry points into the Mira, Cael, and Voss routes.

## S05A — Meeting Mira

Expand the existing meeting behind the warehouses.

### New information

- Mira's missing brother is named Arenn.
- Arenn worked on the foundations of the lighthouse.
- Before disappearing, he stole part of an old tunnel plan.
- Mira has heard his voice beneath the lighthouse on three separate nights.
- The voice knew private memories that only Arenn should know.
- Mira does not know whether the voice is truly her brother or something that learned how to imitate him.

### Final choices

- Take the map and promise to help.
- Demand proof.
- Report the matter to the guard.

The old map remains a critical route item.

## S05B — The Chapel of Salt

Expand the existing conversation with Father Cael.

### New material

- Lio remembers Cael conducting the funeral of Lio's mother.
- Cael argues that not every sacrifice was forced.
- Lio finds old names scratched beneath layers of salt.
- A damaged prayer book contains a removed passage from the old liturgy.
- Cael recognizes Lio's description of the ledger but initially denies it.
- Cael repeatedly looks toward the sealed crypt.

### Final choices

- Tell him about the ledger.
- Ask for a blessing.
- Plan to break into the crypt later.

## S05C — The Harbor Watch Post — New Voss Route Scene

Instead of moving directly to Voss's office, Lio first observes the guard at work.

### Purpose

- Show why ordinary citizens tolerate Voss.
- Demonstrate that the guard genuinely prevents some disasters.
- Establish the mixture of public service and repression.

### Events

- Guards distribute food and organize an evacuation.
- They confiscate lists of missing people.
- A frightened witness is pressured to change a statement.
- A covered convoy departs toward the lighthouse.

### Choices

- Help an injured guard.
- Read restricted incident reports.
- Follow the covered convoy.

These choices should influence Voss respect, Voss suspicion, Reputation, and access to later dialogue.

## S06 — The Child on the Shore

Keep the current encounter with Noa, but expand it.

### New material

- Noa arranges shells into a map of Veyr.
- She knows Lio's name before he introduces himself.
- She says the light cast her out.
- She recognizes the ledger or copied fragment.
- She warns that the lighthouse will choose a new voice on the following night.
- If Lio experienced the dream, she references the sunken bell.

### Final choices

- Help Noa and take her to the chapel.
- Question her about the light.
- Leave her with the guard.

# ACT II — Investigation and Fracturing Alliances

This act should contain the largest expansion. The investigation should be divided into three major strands:

1. historical evidence;
2. living witnesses and relationships;
3. the supernatural nature of the lighthouse.

A player should not be required to see every scene in a single playthrough.

## Historical Evidence Branch

### S07 — The Missing Lamplighter's House — New Scene

Lio and Mira, or Lio alone, search Arenn's room.

### Discoveries

- A damp personal journal.
- A partial mechanical sketch.
- A splinter of white ceremonial metal.
- The date of the next ritual.
- An unsent letter.

### Important revelation

Arenn initially cooperated with the guard because he believed he was maintaining a legitimate protective system. His later entries become frightened and fragmented.

### Possible choices

- Take the full journal.
- Copy only the technical pages.
- Hide evidence from Mira.
- Leave a false trail for the guard.

## S08 — The Forbidden Archive

Retain the current scene, but turn it into a longer investigative sequence.

### Expanded sequence

1. Enter the sealed archive.
2. Search catalogues and false indexes.
3. Compare multiple official versions of the same events.
4. Match disappearance records with lighthouse maintenance.
5. Confirm the nine-year pattern.
6. Find a name personally connected to Lio.

### Personal connection

A victim record may contain the surname of Lio's mother or another close relative. The document should not conclusively prove what happened, but it should give Lio a personal stake in the mystery.

### Final choices

- Take the register of victims.
- Burn selected records to hide the intrusion.
- Leave a sign for Cael.

## S09 — The Crypt Beneath the Chapel — New Scene

Accessible through Cael's trust or through a break-in.

### Contents

- Ritual bowls.
- Clothes belonging to former victims.
- Sealed liturgical records.
- The ceremonial key mechanism.
- A testimony written by the previous priest.

### Cael's confession

Cael may admit that years earlier he helped select or prepare one of the victims. This should explain his high Shame value and make his later public confession meaningful.

### Choices

- Demand every name.
- Protect Cael's secret for now.
- Take the ritual instructions.
- Destroy the evidence of Cael's involvement.

## Mira and Tunnel Branch

### S10 — Warehouse Thirteen — New Scene

Mira introduces Lio to dockworkers, smugglers, and relatives of the missing.

### Purpose

- Show the social cost of the disappearances.
- Build Mira's role as a future political or resistance leader.
- Test whether the group believes Lio.

### Events

- Former lighthouse workers describe voices in the walls.
- A widow shows a returned coat still wet with seawater.
- Someone accuses Lio of informing for Voss.
- The group reveals tide records and hidden access points.

### Choices

- Reveal the ledger.
- Share only part of the truth.
- Defend the guard's actions.
- Offer supplies for an expedition.

## S11 — Tunnels Beneath the Harbor

Keep the existing tunnel scene, but expand the journey.

### Expanded sequence

- Enter through a concealed dockside passage.
- Find traces of a guard convoy.
- Discover Arenn's knife.
- Reach walls covered in repeated warnings:

> Do not look into the light.

- Cross a flooded corridor.
- Hear the many-voiced children's song.

### Choices

- Follow the singing.
- Use Mira's map to bypass the voices.
- Seal the passage and retreat.

## S12 — The Tide Chamber — New Scene

The tunnels open into a chamber where the sea meets the lighthouse foundations.

### Discoveries

- Crystalline channels carry light through the stone.
- Human silhouettes can be seen beneath the water.
- Arenn's voice calls Mira closer.
- Noa, if present or previously trusted, suggests that the voice is made from many people.

### Choices

1. Let Mira attempt to reach Arenn.
2. Break one of the light channels.
3. Mark the chamber and retreat.

### Narrative constraint

Arenn should not be fully rescued or definitively confirmed dead. His fate must remain unresolved so that Mira's grief and anger remain compatible with later continuation routes.

## Voss Branch

### S13 — Voss's Office

Keep the current confrontation, but allow Voss to present a stronger factual case.

### Voss's argument

- Before the pact, Veyr was repeatedly devastated by the fog.
- A previous attempt to extinguish the lighthouse killed hundreds.
- The ritual is rare, controlled, and effective.
- Neighboring cities refused aid.
- Voss personally lost someone during an earlier failure of the light.

He should remain responsible for concealment, coercion, and sacrifice, but his belief that the system saves more lives should feel sincere.

### Existing choices

- Pretend loyalty.
- Accuse him of the sacrifices.
- Steal the white-metal key.

## S14 — Inspection of the Lower Quay — New Scene

Voss takes Lio on an official patrol, or Lio follows him secretly.

### Events

- The light visibly protects some streets from the fog.
- Families thank the guard for evacuating them.
- A sealed house contains the next intended victim.
- Officers falsify reports.
- Voss stops one guard from using unnecessary violence.

### Function

The scene should force the player to hold two facts at once:

- Voss's system saves lives;
- Voss's system is built on hidden victims.

## S15 — The Keeper's Supper — New Scene

A quiet, dialogue-heavy scene.

Voss offers Lio a place in the city administration and access to the real archives.

Suggested line:

> Every rebel believes he is the first person to discover the price of light. I preserve their names.

### Choices

- Accept the offer sincerely.
- Accept as a deception.
- Refuse publicly.
- Ask to see the previous Keeper.

This scene should provide a credible route toward both Order of Voss and the New Keeper ending.

## Noa and Supernatural Branch

### S16 — Night in the Chapel — New Scene

Noa cannot sleep. Salt on the chapel walls forms words.

### Events

- Noa speaks with several voices.
- Cael recognizes one of them.
- Lio learns that Noa is not merely a witness.
- The scene suggests that Noa may have once been selected as a sacrifice, or may carry fragments of several victims.

Her origin must remain unresolved.

### Choices

- Let the voices speak through her.
- Stop the ritual.
- Ask Cael to identify the voices.
- Promise Noa that she will not be taken.

## S17 — Noa's Vision

Keep the current underwater vision, but expand it.

### Images

- Familiar locations appear drowned beneath the sea.
- Former Keepers stand inside columns of light.
- Arenn appears among indistinct faces.
- Lio sees a person connected to his past.
- A second pale lighthouse appears on a distant coast.

The second lighthouse should be a subtle image rather than a complete revelation. It should foreshadow the continuation route beyond Veyr.

### Existing final choices

- Accept the vision to the end.
- Push Noa away and interrupt it.
- Promise that the lighthouse will not take her.

# ACT III — The Night of Choice

The final act should make earlier decisions visible across the city before the player reaches the Lighthouse Heart.

## S18 — Bells Without a Priest — New Scene

The city enters a state of alarm.

### Conditional outcomes

Depending on earlier decisions:

- Cael may warn the public.
- Mira may gather people from the Lower Quay.
- Voss may impose a curfew.
- The guard may actively hunt Lio.
- Noa may disappear and move toward the lighthouse alone.
- Families may begin naming the missing in public.

This scene should reflect values and flags such as Alarm Raised, Reputation, Priest Confessed, Mira Trust, Noa Bond, and Voss Control.

## S19 — The Last Meeting in the Archive — New Scene

The main characters gather before the final approach.

Not every character must appear in every route.

### Possible actions

- Reveal all evidence to everyone.
- Conceal part of the truth.
- Accuse Cael.
- Negotiate with Voss.
- Promise Mira that Arenn will not be abandoned.
- Promise to protect Noa.

### Function

This should be the major relationship-payoff scene before the climax.

## S20 — The Lighthouse Gate

Keep the existing entry choices:

- Enter through the tunnels with Mira.
- Ask Cael to confess publicly.
- Convince Voss that Lio can help.
- Use the stolen key.
- Flee the city.

Expand the confrontation before the choice:

- Voss gives his final warning.
- Mira reveals what she discovered about Arenn.
- Cael admits that he knows the ritual formula.
- Noa says the lighthouse has already chosen.

## S21A — Entry Through the Tunnels — New Route Scene

Mira and Lio enter through the Tide Chamber.

### Consequences

- Supplies may be lost.
- A light channel may be damaged.
- Mira loyalty may increase.
- Arenn's trail reaches the interior of the lighthouse.
- Lio may have to save Mira from the voices.

## S21B — The Procession of Salt — New Route Scene

Cael publicly confesses and leads citizens toward the lighthouse.

### Consequences

- Reputation rises.
- Voss control weakens.
- Panic may increase.
- Citizens witness light leaking from the stone.
- Families begin speaking the victims' names aloud.

This route should strongly prepare the City Without Light continuation.

## S21C — Agreement with the Keeper — New Route Scene

Lio enters openly with Voss.

### Events

- Voss shows Lio the remains or traces of the previous Keeper.
- He explains that the mechanism requires a conscious voice, not merely a body.
- He offers controlled choices:
  - maintain the current system;
  - find a replacement;
  - take the burden personally;
  - attempt to deceive the mechanism.

This route should support both Order of Voss and New Keeper.

## S21D — Pursuit on the Spiral — New Route Scene

Available when the key was stolen or the alarm is high.

### Events

- Lio and any allies are pursued through the interior spiral.
- The lighthouse reacts to the stolen key.
- The player may lose Calm, Light, evidence, or a relationship advantage.
- Noa may guide the group by listening to voices in the walls.

## S22 — The Lighthouse Heart

Keep the current final chamber, but turn it into a full climax.

### Expanded sequence

1. Reveal the structure of bone, glass, salt, and brass.
2. Hear the voices of the victims.
3. Learn that the mechanism requires a conscious voice.
4. Let Mira confront Voss over Arenn.
5. Let Cael confess his role in the previous ritual.
6. Let Noa reveal that the lighthouse may be a lock rather than the true source of the fog.
7. Present the final decision.

The sixth point should remain ambiguous. It creates future narrative space without changing the meaning of the current endings.

### Final choices

- Break the core and reveal the truth.
- Give control to Voss.
- Bind the light to Lio.
- Escape with the victim register.

# Endings

The four current endings remain the canonical branch outcomes.

## S23 — Ending: The Light of Truth

The core is broken and the lighthouse goes dark.

### Expanded outcome

- People outside hear the names or voices of the victims.
- Voss loses control but warns that the coming night may be worse.
- Mira hears Arenn without seeing him.
- Cael begins publicly reciting the names of the sacrificed.
- Noa says the fog has not disappeared; it has only stopped being fed.

### Final state

- The lighthouse is dark.
- The victims are known.
- Veyr survives, but its institutions begin to fracture.
- The city must learn to live without the lie that protected it.

Suggested final line:

> The lighthouse went dark. Veyr survived, but without the lie that had pretended to be light.

This state must remain compatible with **Whisper II-A — City Without Light**.

## S24 — Ending: Voss's Order

Lio gives control to Voss.

### Expanded outcome

- The evidence is sealed again.
- Mira escapes or disappears into the tunnels.
- Cael is forced to perform the ritual.
- Noa is hidden, detained, or monitored depending on earlier choices.
- Lio receives an administrative position.
- New banners bearing the lighthouse symbol appear across the city.

### Final state

- The lighthouse remains active.
- Veyr is safe, orderly, and increasingly authoritarian.
- The truth survives only in fragments and underground networks.

This state must remain compatible with **Whisper II-B — Order of the Lighthouse**.

## S25 — Ending: Escape with the Map

Lio leaves Veyr with evidence.

### Expanded outcome

- Earlier choices determine who helps him escape.
- The map contains symbols connected to other ports.
- The lighthouse beam appears to follow the road.
- A second pale light can be seen on a distant coast.
- Noa may physically accompany Lio, remain in Veyr, or exist only as a voice in memory depending on the route.

### Final state

- Veyr remains behind with the lighthouse still active.
- Lio becomes a fugitive witness.
- The truth is incomplete but portable.
- The mystery expands beyond one city.

This state must remain compatible with **Whisper II-C — The Road Beyond Veyr**.

## S26 — Ending: The New Keeper

Lio binds the light to himself.

### Expanded outcome

- Lio briefly sees every street in Veyr at once.
- He hears the prayers of the entire city.
- He speaks to Mira in his own voice one final time.
- Noa recognizes that part of him remains alive.
- Cael kneels, whether in worship or despair.
- The mechanism becomes calm, but a new hunger begins inside it.

### Final state

- Lio becomes the living heart of the lighthouse.
- The fog retreats.
- Veyr appears saved.
- Lio's humanity begins to erode.
- Mira and Noa remain possible agents of a future rescue or destruction attempt.

This state must remain compatible with **Whisper II-D — Keeper in the Heart**.

## Resource and attribute balancing

The existing visible resources remain suitable:

- Light;
- Reputation;
- Supplies;
- Calm;
- Truth.

The existing character attributes should also remain the baseline.

However, all effects and final thresholds must be rebalanced when the expanded JSON is created. Adding new scenes without rebalancing would make high Truth, Insight, Trust, or Courage values too easy to obtain.

Recommended balancing principles:

- Not every investigative discovery should increase Truth.
- Some discoveries should increase Insight but reduce Calm.
- Public actions should affect Reputation and Voss Control.
- Mira Trust should require consistent behavior rather than one major choice.
- Cael's public confession should require both Trust and Doubt or reduced Shame.
- The New Keeper ending should require the key, sufficient Insight, and the hidden mark from the lighthouse.
- The Light of Truth ending should remain achievable through either strong evidence or a strong bond with Noa, but not trivially through both.

# English-Language Requirement

The future expanded story export must use English for all player-facing narrative content, including:

- project name;
- scene names;
- Scene Group names;
- narration;
- spoken dialogue;
- choice labels;
- unavailable-choice hints;
- resource display names;
- asset display names where they are part of the story presentation.

Internal variable keys and attribute keys may remain concise English identifiers such as `truth`, `sanity`, `mira_route`, and `lio_marked_by_light`.

Existing artwork and asset IDs can be reused. Existing files do not need to be renamed unless Narrium displays their names directly to the player.

## Recommended English title

The working English title should be:

> **Whisper Beneath the Lighthouse**

Alternative title if a more literary tone is desired later:

> **The Whisper Beneath the Lighthouse**

For consistency with the current Polish title structure, **Whisper Beneath the Lighthouse** is recommended.

# Future JSON Generation Constraints

When the expanded Narrium JSON is generated from this outline:

1. Preserve the IDs of existing characters, resources, variables, assets, and endings wherever possible.
2. Preserve the existing four ending scene IDs if practical.
3. Add new UUIDs only for new scenes, pages, choices, effects, conditions, and variables.
4. Reuse existing background assets for expanded versions of existing scenes.
5. New scenes may temporarily reuse thematically appropriate existing backgrounds if dedicated art is not yet available.
6. Keep Scene Groups organizational only; they must not affect runtime logic.
7. Verify that all four endings are reachable.
8. Verify that unavailable-choice hints are accurate.
9. Verify that no route creates impossible negative resource states unless intentionally supported by Narrium.
10. Keep all player-facing content in English.

This outline is the approved narrative basis for the future expanded JSON export of **Whisper Beneath the Lighthouse**.
